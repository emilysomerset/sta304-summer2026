Learning Outcomes
  • Define and identify the key elements of the sampling problem (e.g., target population, sampling frame, sampling units).
  • Define selection bias and describe ways selection bias can occur.
  • Evaluate survey designs for potential sources of selection bias.
  • Define measurement error/bias and describe different sources of response inaccuracy.
  • Evaluate survey questions by identifying the concept of interest, potential sources of response inaccuracy, and rewriting questions to more accurately measure that concept.
  • Understand the difference between sampling and non-sampling errors.
  • Compare the advantages and limitations of a census versus a sample.
  • Understand the difference between probability and non-probability based sampling.
