Course Description

Design of surveys, sources of bias, randomized response surveys. Techniques of sampling: stratification, clustering, unequal probability selection. Sampling inference, estimates of population mean and variances, ratio estimation. Observational data: correlation vs. causation, missing data, sources of bias.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students should be able to:

  • Design surveys and sampling strategies to appropriately gather information of interest, in- cluding selecting and implementing suitable sampling techniques and study designs, while evaluating trade-offs among alternative approaches.
  • Apply a variety of statistical methods to draw valid inferences from data collected through surveys or samples.
  • Evaluate sources of bias in a study and critically assess study design, including its strengths, limitations, and appropriateness of analytical methods.
  • Effectively communicate statistical results and conclusions to both technical and non-technical audiences.
