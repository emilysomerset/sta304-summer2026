Learning Outcomes
  • Understand core probability and statistical concepts (e.g., random variables, estimators, sampling distributions, bias, mean squared error, confidence intervals) used to make inferences from a probability sample.
  • Describe and distinguish common probability sampling designs, including simple random, stratified, cluster, and systematic sampling.
  • Become familiar with standard notation used in sampling theory.
  • Understand when certain sampling techniques are appropriate.
  • Compute the probability of selecting a sample or a unit under simple random sampling with and without replacement.
  • Describe the process of drawing a simple random sampling with and without replacement.
