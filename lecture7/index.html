Learning Outcomes
  • Distinguish between primary sampling units (PSUs) and secondary sampling units (SSUs) in cluster sampling.
  • Distinguish between one stage and two stage cluster sampling designs.
  • Identify key components of a cluster sampling design, including the number of PSUs and SSUs in the population and in the sample.
  • Estimate population means and totals under one stage and two stage cluster sampling designs.
  • Conduct inference for population means and totals under one stage and two stage cluster sampling designs.
  • Estimate the population mean and conduct inference when the total number of SSUs is unknown.
  • Derive the bias and variance of selected estimators.
  • Explain why cluster sampling is often less efficient than simple random sampling due to similarity among units within clusters.
